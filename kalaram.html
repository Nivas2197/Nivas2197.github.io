    ABOUT

    The Kalaram Mandir in Nashik, Maharashtra, is a sacred Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Rama. Located in the Panchavati area, it is a place of deep devotion and striking heritage. The temple gets its name from the black statue ("kala" means black) of Lord Rama housed within.

    Constructed in 1788 by Sardar Rangarao Odhekar, the temple was built with black stone and showcases elegant 18th-century architecture. With its tall spire, gold-plated domes, and peaceful surroundings, Kalaram Mandir attracts thousands of devotees daily.

    The temple's significance also lies in its association with social reformer Dr. B. R. Ambedkar, who led a protest here in 1930 advocating Dalit entry into Hindu temples. Today, it remains a landmark of both religious unity and social history.

    The temple is most vibrant during the Ram Navami festival, when the whole area lights up with processions, devotional singing, and spiritual celebrations.
