    ABOUT

    Perched majestically atop the Sahyadri Mountains, about 35 kilometers southwest of Pune, Sinhagad Fort is a timeless sentinel of Maharashtra‚Äôs glorious past. This fort, once known as Kondhana, is a site of immense historical significance and natural beauty. It has witnessed epic battles, including the legendary Battle of Sinhagad in 1670, where valor, sacrifice, and strategy led to an unforgettable victory for the Marathas. Today, Sinhagad Fort is a paradise for trekkers, history buffs, and nature lovers, offering a mesmerizing blend of history and adventure. Whether you seek breathtaking panoramic views, thrilling trekking trails, or a deep dive into Maharashtra‚Äôs past, Sinhagad promises an experience like no other.

    The History

    Sinhagad Fort has a rich history dating back over 2,000 years. It has changed hands multiple times, with rulers from different dynasties leaving their mark. However, its most famous chapter unfolded in 1670 when Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj orchestrated a daring mission to reclaim the fort from the Mughals.
