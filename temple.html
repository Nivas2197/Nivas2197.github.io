"Maharashtra Temples: Where Divinity Meets Legacy."

SIDDHIVINAYAK TEMPLE, MUMBAI

The Shri Siddhivinayak Ganapati Mandir is a Hindu temple dedicated to Ganesha. Located in Prabhadevi, Mumbai, it was built by Laxman Vithu and Deubai Patil in 1801. It is one of Mumbai's most popular Hindu temples.

VISIT

SAI BABA MANDIR, NASHIK

Shree Saibaba Sansthan Trust, Shirdi, manages the Samadhi Temple and premises, devoted to the teachings of Sai Baba, "Sabka Malik Ek". It provides amenities to millions of visiting devotees.

VISIT

SHIV MANDIR, AMBERNATH

The historic 11th-century Shiv Mandir at Ambarnath, also called the Ambreshwar Shiva Temple, sits by the Waldhuni river, 2 km from Ambarnath railway station (East).

VISIT

BHADRAMARUTI MANDIR, RATNAGIRI

The Bhadra Maruti Temple, located near Ellora Caves, features Lord Hanuman in a rare sleeping posture. It is one of only three temples showcasing Hanuman in this form.

VISIT

KALARAM MANDIR, NASHIK

Built in 1782 by Sardar Rangrao Odhekar, Kalaram Mandir marks the spot where Lord Ram is believed to have lived during his exile. It is a masterpiece of temple architecture.

VISIT

KHANDOBA MANDIR, JEJURI

Around 50 km from Pune lies Jejuri, the prime worship center of Khandoba, a deity revered by warriors, farmers, and priests across Maharashtra.
